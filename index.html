Linus Benedict Torvalds

The creator of the Linux kernel and Git
IEEE Consumer Electronics Award (Ibuka Award) certificate as presented to Linus Torvalds 2018 at CES / IEEE ICCE in Las Vegas
Linus Torvalds is a Finnish-American software engineer best known for creating the Linux kernel, which became the foundation for the Linux operating system. He also created Git, the widely-used version control system that revolutionized software development workflows. Torvalds is one of the most influential figures in modern computing and open-source software.
Timeline of Linus Torvalds' Life and Work
  1. 1969 - Born on December 28 in Helsinki, Finland.
  2. 1981 - Started programming at age 11 on a Commodore VIC-20.
  3. 1991 - While at the University of Helsinki, released the first version of the Linux kernel as a hobby project.
  4. 1994 - Version 1.0 of the Linux kernel is released.
  5. 1997 - Moved to California and began working for Transmeta Corporation.
  6. 2005 - Created Git, a fast, distributed version control system, after a fallout with BitKeeper (which Linux used before).
  7. 2012 - Awarded the Millennium Technology Prize for his contributions to open-source software.
  8. 2018 - Linus Torvalds received the IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award. This award recognizes his leadership in the development and proliferation of the Linux operating system.
